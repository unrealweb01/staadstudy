Wind Load Analysis Components

A simple guide to understanding wind load coefficients in building design.

1) CPE (External Pressure Coefficient - 6.2.3)

Represents the wind force acting on the exterior surfaces of a building.

Calculation:

  • Walls (Clause 6.2.3.1): For walls of rectangular clad buildings, refer to Table 5.
  • Pitched, Hipped, and Monoslope Roofs (Clause 6.2.3.2): Use Table 6 for coefficients based on roof slope and wind direction.
  • Canopy Roofs (Clause 6.2.3.3): Check Tables 8 and 9 for double and mono-pitch canopy roofs.

2) CPI (Internal Pressure Coefficient)

Describes the wind force that permeates through openings (e.g., doors, windows) into the building. It may be positive or negative.

Calculation:

  • Small Openings (≤ 5% of wall area): Use Cpi = ±0.2.
  • Medium Openings (5-20% of wall area): Use Cpi = ±0.5.
  • Large Openings (> 20% of wall area): Use Cpi = ±0.7.
